Project Overview

Bringing the community back into community-based fitness

Background

In 2020, the pandemic hit and changed the trajectory of traditional fitness for the worse; research has shown that 9 out of 10 people say they plan to continue working out at home post-pandemic. How do we bridge the gap between traditional solutions and at-home solutions so that fitness enthusiasts are still meeting their goals?

By building in community support by design, we can make sure that fitness is accessible, motivating, and most importantly, providing a sense of community and belonging.

Our high-level goals

  • Make it easy for everyone and anyone to use at their fingertips
  • Build in community support
  • Create a platform that keeps people coming back

Company

MotivateU, a fitness technology start-up

Problem

There’s a gap between traditional solutions and AI solutions for fitness enjoyers

MotivateU (MU) was created to bridge the gap between traditional fitness solutions and AI solutions for fitness enjoyers in order to bring back the community in community based fitness. We observed that current products and services are expensive, require additional hardware, inflexible, or leave out the main reason people work out in the first place: for the community. ​

This is causing fitness enthusiasts to stop working out as their needs aren’t being met.

How might we improve the current fitness services and solutions so that our customers are successful in achieving their fitness goals?

Research Insights

Determining what is and isn't working in the industry

From the research conducted, we found 3 main frustrations that fitness enthusiasts had when it came to workout options: ​

1

Current solutions aren’t flexible

Fitness enthusiasts don’t want to go in-person to the gyms due to the pandemic. This left at-home solutions such as Peloton or Mirror but they felt the equipment would be “heavy and hard to set up” as well as “expensive”.

2

Data-driven but have no human interaction

While current solutions provide feedback and data results on workout sessions, fitness enthusiasts often only interact with a bot and never a real human.

3

Lack of community leading to lack of motivation

Fitness enthusiasts “don’t enjoy doing a lot of exercising by [themselves]” and find that “keeping a fitness routine can be difficult sometimes” because of it.

Pain Point 1

Keeping on the go

When asked, users said they owned some sort of smart device that has a camera, allowing them to bring these devices on the go wherever and whenever they went. We decided an app that can be opened on a desktop or on your mobile device would be the best option to accommodate our users.

Knowing this, I made sure to design responsively using a 12 column grid system so that users are able to use the web app fluidly on desktop or mobile. With that in mind, I took a mobile first design approach to focus on the essentials needed for the app and scaled up to desktop.

Pain Point 2

Real humans with real results

Here, I focused on user accuracy and how to display their progress so that users can work out in front of a live trainer and receive real-time feedback and data-driven metrics from MU's patent-pending technology.

Each person’s video feed would be enclosed in a thin, colored outline that would match the accuracy level at the time of the session. Considering accessibility and visual impairments, a number value is calculated and displayed with a matching color bar at the bottom of the screen. This bar can be opened or closed to view a real-time graph of your movement at any time.

Pain Point 3

We’re all in this together

We wanted to build in community support by design. With blue and it's variants being the primary color of the app, it gives a sense of trust and security as well as connection and community that users feel is lacking in current solutions. There was also a deliberate choice to have ample white space to make the app feel open and balanced.

In regards to the reward system, we decided to use the motif of high fives rather than a thumbs up or “upvote” as a way to motivate and reward each others’ success. Simulating a high five in this way celebrates connectedness with one another even when not in the same physical space. It’s a great way to increase cooperation and inspire trust with others within the community and platform.

Impact

Outcome & what's next

Within our first month of launching the beta product, our cumulative revenue was over $27,000 with a month-over-month (MoM) growth rate of 825%. We had a waitlist of over 500 excited users who were ready to take on the challenge of completing their fitness goals.

If I were to do things differently next time, I would

  • Dedicate more time to understanding the business goals of the organization
  • Conduct user testing and garner feedback in order to validate and iterate on designs
  • Work more directly with the research and engineering teams
  • Ensure designs are consistent across the board
